Bio

Natalie Giarratano received her MFA and PhD in creative writing from Western Michigan University. Her first collection of poems, Leaving Clean, won the 2013 Liam Rector First Book Prize in Poetry and will be published in May 2013 (Briery Creek Press). Recent poems appear in American Literary Review, Laurel Review, and Hayden’s Ferry Review, among others. D.A. Powell selected her work for inclusion in the 2011 edition of Best New Poets, and Lynn Thompson selected her work as the winner of the 2011 Ann Stanford Poetry Prize from Southern California Review. She co-founded and co-edits Pilot Light—an online journal of 21st century poetics and criticism, teaches writing at American University, and lives in northern Virginia with her husband, Zach Green, and their pup, Miles.
